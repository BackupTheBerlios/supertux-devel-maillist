sik0fewl just brought up that the filesize of the supertux tarball is
a little extra-large and I agree. A quick look tells us that 24MB are
music, which seems rather wastefull given that the rest is just 12MB
(and even that could probally be cut down quite a bit):

-rw-r--r-- 1 ingo ingo 12M 2007-01-06 08:25 supertux-0.3.0-without-music.tar.bz2
-rw-r--r-- 1 ingo ingo 36M 2007-01-06 08:22 supertux-0.3.0.tar.bz2

A quick brute force attempt reencoding the music with the lowest
quality setting brought quite a good improvment, havn't tested with
all files, just the forest.ogg one (from 3MB down 1.2MB):

-rw-r--r-- 1 ingo ingo 2985475 2006-12-18 00:22 forest-original.ogg
-rw-r--r-- 1 ingo ingo 1176902 2007-01-06 08:32 forest-lowest.ogg

Sound quality still sounded quite ok, with no noticable artefacts.

Another way would of course be to just use the MIDI files directly in
the game and thus cut down the size of the music to almost none, sound
quality would of course suffer a bit then.
