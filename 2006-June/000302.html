Hmm... nevermind. From what I have gathered while trying to get to run 
it, Flexlay is *evil*. Compilation is failure-fraught, the required 
development version of ClanLib (one of its dependencies) tends to be 
broken, etc. If you run Windows, you will never, I repeat, NEVER get it 
running, unless you have the brainpower of Steven Hawking. Windows has, 
in my opinion, always been a bitch to native C/C++ developers.

Once we get Milestone 2 running, we'll be better off. The editors being 
worked on (SuperTux#-Editor and Cobble) are written using .NET's C# (the 
former runs on Mono on non-Windows systems) and work much better. (Sorry 
for raving about Milestone 2 and how good it is while everybody is 
already eagerly waiting for it... I need something to contrast Flexlay 
to.) Unfortunately, they are not compatible with Milestone 1's level format.

If, for any reason, you are tired of living... 
http://flexlay.berlios.de/. However, editing the level files in a text 
editor proves to be much easier, even if you hate editing in text editors.
